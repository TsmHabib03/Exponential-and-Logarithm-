Exponential Models

An exponential model is a function reflecting a quantity which grows or decays at a rate proportional to its current value. The functions are of the form y = a(b) where a is the initial amount and b is the rate of growth or decay.

Zeros of an Exponential Function

The zero of an exponential function is the point where the graph crosses the x-axis. The zero can be computed by equating the function value to zero. Just like how we do it with other functions. Note: Not all exponential functions have zeros.

Logarithm

A logarithm is the exponent or power to which a base number must be raised to produce a given number

Logarithmic laws

The logarithm laws are the rules by which logarithms may be combined, and are derived from the definition of the logarithm and the index laws.

Solving logarithmic equation

To solve a logarithmic equation, you typically use logarithmic properties to simplify and isolate the logarithmic expression. Then, you can convert the equation to an exponential form to find the variable.

The change of base formula allows you to convert a logarithm with one base to a logarithm with a different base

Power Rule (Logarithmic)

Interest

In finance and economics, interest is payment from a debtor or deposit-taking financial institution to a lender or depositor of an amount above repayment of the principal sum, at a particular rate. It is distinct from a fee which the borrower may pay to the lender or some third party.

Simple Interest

Interest is the cost of borrowing money, expressed as a percentage of the total amount of the loan. Simple interest is an annual percentage of the amount borrowed, referred to as the annual interest rate.
